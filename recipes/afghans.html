Afghans

Description

An Afghan is a traditional New Zealand biscuit made from flour, butter, cornflakes, sugar and cocoa powder, topped with chocolate icing and a half walnut.

Ingredients

  • 200g butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup of caster sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • 1 1/4 cups of standard flour
  • 1/4 cup of cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 cups of cornflakes

    • Icing

  • 1 1/2 cups of icing sugar
  • 1 Tbsp of cocoa powder
  • 1 Tbsp of butter
  • 2-3 Tbsp of boiling water
  • Walnut halves to decorate

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 180Â°C bake. Line two large baking trays with baking paper.
  2. In a large bowl, beat butter, caster sugar and vanilla until light and fluffy.
  3. Sift in the flour and cocoa. Stir until mostly combined, then add the cornflakes and mix well.
  4. Roll tablespoons of mixture into balls and place on prepared trays. Press down lightly with a fork.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, until biscuits are just firm to the touch. Leave on trays for a few minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Icing

  1. Sift icing sugar and cocoa into a bowl.
  2. Add butter and 1 tablespoon boiling water at a time, mixing well and adding more hot water as required until icing is a spreadable consistency.
  3. Ice cooled biscuits and decorate with walnut halves, if desired.
